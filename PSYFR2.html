How to read the serpent · formulas to pop into the fields

A quick reference for casting NATORI-ON-PSYFR: the workflow, the vocabulary, a cookbook of copy-ready operations, and answers to the questions that come up first. Tap any formula to copy it, then paste into the engine's Custom operation field.

§1 · Quick Start

Cast in four moves

Seed your anchors

In The Oracle, add two or more anchor dates — type them in, or scroll to The Ledger and hit seed ⚓ on any documented event. Two anchors is the minimum; more anchors = more interval pairs to project from.

Choose your operations

The 19-Operation Pack loads by default. Swap it for a themed pack (138, 19 Metonic, Phoenix Lattice, Golden) with the violet buttons, or paste your own from the cookbook below.

Cast the Oracle

Press ⟳ Cast the Oracle. Every anchor-pair interval is run through every operation, and each projected date is scored against the chronology. Switch the scoring lens (V8 / V7) to re-rank live.

Read the agreement

Scroll to The Convergence — where several independent operations land on the same date. That agreement is the real signal. Click any result to read it on The Wheels.

The one habit that matters

After a cast, hit ✦ NOVEL ONLY to hide the ECHO rows (projections that just land back on an input). What remains are the dates the chronology didn't already hand you — that's where the discoveries live.

§2 · The Vocabulary

Seven words to know

X-Date

An anchor — a known date you feed the engine. Every cast needs at least two.

Y · the interval

The number of axial rotations (days) between a pair of X-Dates. Counting turns of the Earth, not years — the Archaix way.

Operation

An equation that transforms Y into a day-offset. Always begins X1+ or X2+.

Z-Date

A projected date — the output. Offset applied to X1 or X2, then read across every calendar.

Echo

A Z-Date that lands back on an input anchor (±1 day). A confirmation, not a discovery — dimmed and tagged.

Convergence

When several different operations agree on one date. The strongest signal in the whole instrument.

MSRF

The resonance number-set (138, 831, 19 …). A projection whose interval or offset matches one scores extra.

§4 · The Grammar & Constants

What's legal in an operation

Three rules: it must start with X1+ or X2+; it may use only Y, numbers, the constants, the oph_ functions, and arithmetic + − * / ( ); and it must evaluate to a number. Anything else is rejected.

TokenMeaning
X1 / X2The earlier / later anchor of the pair. Your equation begins with one of them, plus an offset.
YThe interval between the two anchors, in days (axial rotations).
OPH_PHIGolden ratio — 1.61803398875
OPH_PIArchaix π — 3.14
OPH_CRVThe curvature constant — 5.08
oph_flip(n)Reverse the digits — 138 → 831, 19 → 91. The palindrome maker.
oph_round(n)Nearest whole day. Use it to keep offsets clean.
oph_floor / oph_ceilRound down / up to a whole day.
oph_abs / oph_sqrtAbsolute value / square root (of the absolute value).

Tip: a bare x between terms is read as multiply, the same as *. So X2+YxOPH_PI and X2+Y*OPH_PI are identical.

§7 · FAQ

Questions that come up first

Why do my top results all say ECHO?

Because one of your anchors is reproducible by the operations. If you seed 2040 and an operation projects right back onto 2040, that's an echo — true, but circular. It's the engine confirming its own input.

Hit ✦ NOVEL ONLY to hide echoes, or remove the anchor that's being re-derived and re-cast to hunt for genuinely new convergences.

What's the difference between the V8 and V7 scoring lenses?

V8 · Chronology-first (default) rewards landing on the real timeline — Phoenix nodes and documented events score highest. Use it to ask "which projections resonate with history?"

V7 · Numbers-first lets the sacred numbers lead — 138 in the digits, palindromes, and MSRF outrank a documented cataclysm. Use it to ask "which projections resonate with the signatures?" The same cast can rank completely differently under each.

What does convergence "strength" mean, and which window should I use?

Strength is the number of distinct operations that independently landed in the same window. Strength 4 means four different equations agree — far stronger than any single high-scoring projection.

Start at ± 30 days. Tighten to exact day for the rarest coincidences; widen to Same year to see which years the whole pack points at. Wider windows find more (and looser) agreement.

Why don't BC dates get eclipse tags?

The precomputed eclipse tables cover roughly 1–3000 CE. Projections outside that range simply carry no eclipse tag — the engine degrades gracefully rather than guessing. Most future-facing casts (2040, 2046 …) are well inside coverage.

Are the dates actually correct per the Chronicon?

Yes — the date engine was verified against every known Archaix anchor before release: today reads AM 5920 / LC 5138, the Great Flood lands on AM 1656, Petrie's survey year on AM 5776, and all 24 Phoenix events sit exactly on the 138-lattice (mod 138 = 108). The šar is reckoned as a turning of the stars — a day — per Breshears.

Can I save a setup and reuse it?

Yes. ⤓ Save .json writes your whole cast — anchors, operations, scoring lens, and convergence window — to a file. ⤒ Load reads one back. It's the same portable model as the original Ophis .oph files, so you can keep a 138-pack, a 19-pack, and a Phoenix-pack as named configurations and share them.

How do I read a result on the calendars?

Click any row in the results or convergence table, or any ledger entry. The date is sent to The Wheels, where all seven reckonings re-read that moment — Phoenix, Nemesis, NER, Annus Mundi, Mayan Long-Count, Anunna turnings, and the Metonic moon. You can also drag the dial directly.

Is this asserting the model as science?

No. This is a worldbuilding and study instrument that renders Jason Breshears' Archaix thesis as living predictive calendrics. The chronology, the šar-as-day reckoning, and the cycle lattice are presented as that thesis — not as established archaeology. Deep-past conversions are cycle-true rather than historically observed.

Nothing happens when I cast — what's wrong?

You need at least two active anchors (the ✓ box lit) and at least one active operation. If the results are empty after a cast, the filter may be too tight — click ALL, or widen the convergence window. The engine also skips intervals over ~3,000,000 days and projections outside roughly 5400 BC – 4000 CE.

Keyboard & reading

Tab moves between every control; Space or Enter toggles the ✓ checkboxes and buttons. The ☾/☀ switch flips light/dark (it follows your system on first visit), and A− / A / A+ scales everything — both remembered between sessions. Reduced-motion settings are honoured.
