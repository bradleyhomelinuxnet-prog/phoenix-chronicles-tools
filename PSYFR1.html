IV · Method & lineage

How the cast is weighed

NATORI-ON-PSYFR fuses two instruments: the Ophis predictive grammar (X-Dates → Y interval → operations → Z-Dates) and the Chronicon calendrics (the Breshears cycle lattice). The fusion is the scoring — and you can switch the lens.

The scoring rules, constants & the echo filter what V8 / V7 reward, point by point

V8 · Chronology-first (default)

Rewards projections that land on the real timeline:

  • Phoenix node (mod 138 = 108) +5 · ±2 yr +2
  • Documented event exact year +5
  • Palindrome (year / AM / Julian-day) +3
  • Metonic-19 to today +2 · 138 in digits +2
  • MSRF on interval/offset +2 · 19 in digits +1
  • Nemesis · NER · baktun edge +1 each

ECHO & the novel filter

A projection that lands back on one of your input anchors (±1 day) is an ECHO — a confirmation, not a discovery. It still scores, but it's dimmed and tagged. The ✦ NOVEL ONLY filter hides echoes so you see only the dates the chronology doesn't already name.

19 is in the resonance set at your request — it is not in the stock Ophis MSRF table.
